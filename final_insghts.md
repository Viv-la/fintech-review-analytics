# Final Insights and Recommendations

# 1. Overview

This analysis examined customer reviews from three Ethiopian mobile banking applications:
- Commercial Bank of Ethiopia (CBE)
- Bank of Abyssinia (BOA)
- Dashen Bank

The project combined web scraping, sentiment analysis, thematic analysis, data visualization, and PostgreSQL database engineering to identify customer satisfaction drivers, recurring pain points, and opportunities for product improvement.

A total of 1180 customer reviews were analyzed.

---

# 2. Key Cross-Bank Findings

## General Customer Sentiment
The majority of customer reviews across all three applications were positive, indicating that Ethiopian users generally appreciate the convenience and accessibility of mobile banking platforms.

Dashen Bank and CBE recorded the strongest positive sentiment levels, while BOA demonstrated comparatively higher negative and neutral sentiment proportions.

---

# 3. Major Satisfaction Drivers

## 3.1 Ease of Use and Convenience
Many users praised:
- easy navigation
- simple interfaces
- convenient transactions
- accessibility of banking services

Users frequently described the applications as:
- “easy”
- “simple”
- “fast”
- “convenient”

This indicates that usability and accessibility are major contributors to customer satisfaction.

---

## 3.2 Successful Transactions and Reliability
Positive reviews often referenced:
- smooth transfers
- successful payments
- quick service delivery

Customers highly value transaction reliability and service consistency.

Applications that minimized transaction failures generally received stronger positive sentiment.

---

# 4. Major Pain Points

## 4.1 Login and Account Access Issues
A recurring complaint across all three banks involved:
- login failures
- PIN problems
- fingerprint authentication issues
- account access challenges

This suggests that authentication and account access systems remain a major friction point for users.

---

## 4.2 OTP and Verification Problems
Many negative reviews referenced:
- delayed OTP delivery
- failed verification codes
- SMS delivery problems

These issues directly affect customer trust and transaction completion.

---

## 4.3 App Reliability and Performance
Users repeatedly mentioned:
- application crashes
- slow loading
- failed transactions
- network-related errors

Technical instability significantly impacts customer experience and increases frustration levels.

---

# 5. Bank-Specific Insights

## 5.1 Commercial Bank of Ethiopia (CBE)

### Strengths
- Strong positive sentiment levels
- Positive feedback on transaction convenience
- Relatively lower negative sentiment

### Challenges
- Login and OTP-related complaints
- Performance issues during transactions

### Recommendations
- Improve authentication stability
- Optimize transaction processing speed
- Strengthen OTP delivery reliability

---

## 5.2 Bank of Abyssinia (BOA)

### Strengths
- Positive reviews regarding functionality and convenience
- Strong adoption among mobile banking users

### Challenges
- Highest negative sentiment among the three banks
- More complaints regarding technical reliability
- Higher concentration of low ratings

### Recommendations
- Prioritize technical issue resolution
- Improve app stability and responsiveness
- Strengthen customer support responsiveness
- Reduce transaction failures and loading delays

---

## 5.3 Dashen Bank

### Strengths
- Highest positive sentiment levels
- Strong customer perception regarding usability
- Positive reviews on overall service experience

### Challenges
- Some complaints related to app crashes and OTP delays

### Recommendations
- Maintain strong user experience standards
- Continue optimizing reliability
- Introduce new customer-requested features

---

# 6. Strategic Recommendations

## Short-Term Priorities
- Resolve recurring login and OTP failures
- Improve transaction speed and reliability
- Enhance customer support responsiveness

## Medium-Term Priorities
- Introduce advanced authentication options
- Improve app performance optimization
- Expand feature personalization

## Long-Term Opportunities
- AI-powered customer support assistants
- Predictive issue monitoring
- Customer behavior analytics dashboards

---

# 7. Ethical Considerations

This project used publicly available customer reviews from the Google Play Store. No personally identifiable customer information was collected or processed.

The analysis focused on aggregated customer experience patterns rather than individual-level profiling.

---

# 8. Conclusion

The analysis demonstrates that customer reviews provide valuable insight into the strengths and weaknesses of Ethiopian mobile banking applications.

While users generally appreciate the convenience of digital banking services, recurring technical issues — particularly around login systems, OTP verification, and transaction reliability — remain significant customer pain points.

By addressing these operational and technical challenges, Ethiopian banks can significantly improve customer trust, retention, and digital banking adoption.
